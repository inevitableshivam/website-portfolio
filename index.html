How a video gets made

Four stages from kickoff to delivery. Every stage has a checkpoint — you see the work before we move on.
Stage 01

Scripting

We start by understanding your product and the outcome the video needs to drive — a demo, a feature explainer, a launch hook. The script gets written around that goal. You approve every line before a single frame is built.

Stage 02

Storyboarding

Storyboards are built in Figma. Every scene is shown to you before it's animated. You can react to a single frame instead of waiting for a full render — small course-corrections, no big surprises.

Stage 03

Sound design

Voiceover comes first — recorded human VO when you want it, or ElevenLabs synthesis when speed matters. Then background music to set tone, and SFX layered scene by scene to give weight to every transition and beat.

Stage 04

Animation

Built in After Effects with kinetic typography, proper easing, and the small detail work that separates produced from rendered. Every keyframe is tuned so the motion feels intentional — not template, not stock.
